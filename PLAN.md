# Plan: Unify the streak rule and single-source calendar-date math

_Locked via grill — by Claude + kiddulu916._

## Goal

The streak transition rule exists twice: `nextStreak()` (`lib/domain/models/streak.dart`, pure, freeze-aware, UTC-safe) drives the headline daily-active streak, while `GameCubit._recordCompletion` (`lib/application/game_cubit.dart:675-706`) re-implements the same yesterday/+1/reset rule inline for per-tier streaks — computing "yesterday" with `DateTime.parse(_date).subtract(Duration(days: 1))`, the exact local-time DST off-by-one that candidate #3 fixed in `previousUtcDay` but which survives here. A third copy of related calendar math lives in `LeaderboardScreen`'s `LeaderboardPeriod.range` (`leaderboard_screen.dart:37-38`): the weekly range computes Monday with the same DST-buggy local subtract, despite its comment claiming it "matches the range used by checkWeeklyPrizes" (which is UTC-safe since #3 — the two can disagree on a DST boundary, showing a leaderboard week that mismatches the prize week). Unify: per-tier streaks route through `nextStreak(hasFreeze: false)` (policy difference preserved — grill decision), and the duplicated calendar arithmetic single-sources into `lib/domain/date_utils.dart`. Pure client-side (streaks/stats/UI never touch the move log, `BoardState.score`, or the TS mirror) — no fixture involvement, no season bump.

## Key decisions & tradeoffs

- **Mechanism unified, policy preserved** (grill decision): per-tier streaks keep no-freeze (`hasFreeze: false`) — the freeze-token economy shields only the headline streak, exactly as the code comments document as intentional. Rejected: extending freeze to per-tier (a product change touching token consumption, achievements, and economy design — not a refactor).
- **Calendar helpers single-sourced, but only the duplicated ones** (grill decision + ladder): `parseUtcDate`, `previousUtcDay`, `mondayOfWeek` have 2-3 consumers each and two live DST bugs among them; the prize-period statics have one consumer and stay put. No speculative `addDays`/`weekRange` API.
- **`previousUtcDay` moves with direct imports, no shim** (Codex round 2): one definition in `date_utils.dart`, every consumer imports it from there; `streak.dart` imports it for `nextStreak`'s own use only.
- **One Monday helper, two documented periods**: leaderboard weekly (current Monday→today) and prize weekly (previous completed Mon→Sun) are different periods by design that now consume the same `mondayOfWeek` sub-rule; the corrected comments say exactly that, and tests assert the sub-rule only.
- **No TS mirror, no season bump**: streaks and leaderboard display ranges are client-only; `verifyRun`/`verifyRunChallenge` never see them. (The server's `leaderboard_period` RPC receives whatever range the client asks for — fixing the client's Monday fixes what's asked.)

## Risks / open questions

- `GameCubit` gains an import of `domain/models/streak.dart` — domain-ward dependency, always allowed (application → domain).
- The DST equivalence claim (old inline rule vs `nextStreak`) is exact on all non-DST inputs and *differs deliberately* on DST-boundary inputs (that's the bug fix) — pinned by the TZ-controlled CI step (per-test TZ injection remains impossible in-process; process-level TZ on Ubuntu CI is the workable substitute).
- `mondayOfWeek` for the leaderboard uses "week containing today" while prizes use "previous completed week" — different periods by design, same Monday sub-rule; the tests assert the sub-rule, not period equality.

## Out of scope

- No freeze-policy changes; no achievement or economy changes.
- No changes to `nextStreak`'s rules or `StreakResult`.
- `NotificationService`'s local-time scheduling (deliberately local — reminders fire at local clock times).
- No TS mirror or season bump (client-only; replay untouched).
- Prize-period statics in `EngagementCubit` beyond the two helper swaps.
